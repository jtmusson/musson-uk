Something to do (2)

I hope you have read John 13:1-17. You will probably have written down something like this:

Q1. What was the original meaning?

a. Why did the writer write this book?
     The key verses here are John 20:30-31, where John tells us why he wrote his Gospel.
     But another answer is fine too: John wrote because he wanted us to know the truth about Jesus.
     This does not mean that I am going to read John 20:30-31 whenever I preach from John’s Gospel!
     But in my preparation this will help me to understand the original meaning of the passage I am going to teach.

b. Why did he write these verses?
     John doesn’t tell us in as many words. But there are a number of possible answers.
     As he begins to tell us about the full extent of Jesus’ love (v1), John shows us that
     Jesus’ greatness, far from excluding servanthood, actually involves it.
     This is so different from the usual human understanding of greatness.

c. What do the individual words mean?
     I am going to have to think about the meaning of the following words, phrases
     and concepts (and you may have found more than I have listed here):
     v1   Passover; the hour
     v2   the devil had prompted Judas
     v5   footwashing
     v8   ‘you have no part with me’
     v10  a bath; clean
     v14  ‘you should wash one another’s feet’
     v17  blessed
