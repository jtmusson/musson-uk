Something to do (9)

Here are two suggestions of how the introduction to a Bible talk on John 13:1-17 might look.

You will notice that one of these suggestions builds the bridge from the Bible passage to the listeners, while the other builds it from the listeners to the Bible passage.

The problem with both these suggestions is that they are not linked to a specific structure-with-headings.


Suggestion One

Chapter 13 of the Gospel is the beginning of the last section of the story John wants to tell us.
     This is the climax.
     We have seen and heard Jesus in action.
     Now we are going to look at the final stage of the story: Jesus is going to his death.

And how does John begin?
     He shows us that the Lord of glory was willing to wash his disciples’ feet.
     And that those of us who follow him should do the same.


Suggestion Two

Have you ever asked yourself this question?
     I trust in Jesus. Well, how does he want me to live?
     That’s what this passage in John’s Gospel is about.
     If you are a Christian and you want to live for Jesus, this is the passage to look at.

Chapter 13 is the beginning of the last stage of the Jesus story.
     Jesus is soon going to be arrested, tried and crucified.
     But first John tells us how Jesus followers should live.
     And he does that by showing us Jesus washing his disciples’ feet.
