Something to do (8)

Your structure-with-headings for John 13:1-17 will doubtless be different from the one I am using here – at least your headings will be. But by looking at this example you will see things that should certainly be included in the main body of your Bible talk.

1. Watch him (1-5)
     a. What must I explain?
          Jesus’ hour
          he loved them to the end
          the devil had prompted Judas to betray Jesus
          what footwashing is

     b. What examples could I use?
          the school headteacher cleaning the school toilets

     c. What application will I bring?
          Do you see who Jesus is and what he decides to do?
          Do you believe that Jesus knows all the things John mentions here?

2. Receive him (6-11)
     a. What must I explain?
          ‘Unless I wash you, you will have no part with me’
          having a bath

     b. What examples could I use?
          Sometimes we are unwilling to accept help because the person offering us help is so important
          one or two examples of this

     c. What application will I bring?
          Are you willing to let Jesus wash you, to ‘give you a bath’?
          Will you ask Jesus to do this for you?

3. Follow him (12-17)
     a. What must I explain?
          Teacher and Lord
          what Jesus means by us washing one another’s feet
          what it means to be blessed

     b. What examples could I use?
          doing something menial for people we consider less important than we are
          one or two examples of this

     c. What application will I bring?
          serving in your family: washing up, looking after the children, shopping
          serving at work: helping a junior colleague with a difficult piece of work
          serving in the church: visiting shut-ins, making coffee, helping with cleaning
