Something to do (6)

There are two main ways to divide up John 13:1-17:

Option One: verses 1-5   Option Two: verses 1-3
  verses 6-11     verses 4-11
  verses 12-17     verses 12-17


The advantage of Option Two is that it puts all the footwashing in the middle section (v4-11). But the disadvantage is that it starts a section in the middle of a sentence.

So although you can make an argument for both options, I think that on balance Option One is better. Most of my suggestions for headings will be for Option One, but I have thrown in a couple of examples for Option Two.

One more thing about the structure of the passage. It would be possible to make verse 1 a section of its own. Feel free to do that if you would like to. But remember that that will mean that you are going to be looking for four headings!

If you have found it difficult to find headings, try to resist looking at the suggestions below until you have tried using these ideas:
     Look for three headings, with each heading consisting of three words.
          The first two words of each heading could be Jesus the
          Or the first two words of each heading could be What Jesus

You will like some of these suggestions more than others. I don’t have a problem with that!

a. 1. The reason (1-5)
  2. The necessity (6-11)
  3. The consequences (12-17)
   
b. 1. Jesus knows (1-3)
  2. Jesus acts (4-11)
  3. Jesus explains (12-17)
   
c. 1. Love is an action (1-5)
  2. Love is a gift (6-11)
  3. Love is a duty (12-17)

d. Three principles
  1. Watch him (1-5)
  2. Receive him (6-11)
  3. Follow him (12-17)
   
e. Three descriptions of what happens in the text
  1. Jesus’ love (1-5)
  2. Peter’s objection (6-11)
  3. Our obligation (12-17)
   
f. Three questions
  1. Who is Jesus? (1-3)
  2. What does Jesus do? (4-11)
  3. What does Jesus want? (12-17)
   
g. Three answers to one question
  The question: How can we be happy? (verse 17)
  1. Watch him (1-5)
  2. Receive him (6-11)
  3. Follow him (12-17)
